What is the structure of your dataset?¶

Before cleaning and aggregating there were over 1.4 million flights over the two year period with 27 features including origin, destination, scheduled times, actual times and various delay times. After aggregation there are approximately 1 million records with 14 features. The aggregate delay times are quantitative while remaining columns are categorical such as carrier, origin and destination.

What is/are the main feature(s) of interest in your dataset?¶

I'm intrested in determining if there are certain characteristics such as day of the week, month, origin, or destination that correlate with delays.

What features in the dataset do you think will help support your investigation into your feature(s) of interest?¶

I expect that winter months will have the highest correlation to delays. I suspect that certain busy days of the week might also be a factor.

Discuss the distribution(s) of your variable(s) of interest. Were there any unusual points? Did you need to perform any transformations?¶

The delays were fairly right skewed and required scaling to produce a normal distribution. I used numpy.log() which produced a reasonably normal distribution for departure delays but did not work quite as well for arrival delays due to there being no arrival delays less than 15 minutes. It is really the arrival delays that matter since those can lead to missed connections among other things.

Of the features you investigated, were there any unusual distributions? Did you perform any operations on the data to tidy, adjust, or change the form of the data? If so, why did you do this?¶

There were a fair amount of short departure delays that were most likely not even noticed by normal consumers. These could probably be omitted to further improve the normal distribution of departure delays. There were also certain carriers that had serveral times more departure delays that likely operate more flights.

Talk about some of the relationships you observed in this part of the investigation. How did the feature(s) of interest vary with other features in the dataset?¶

I found that flight distance had a negative correlation with delays. It was hard to see due to overplotting until a very small transparency value was used. Major airlines like American, United and Delta operate more short to mid range flights. The number of trips a carrier makes between origin and destination has some correlation to delays. Flights on Saturdays have an unusually lower change of being delayed.

The strongest relationship with delays seems to be the number of flights operated in a day followed by carrier. The month has some relationship as well. Were there any interesting or surprising interactions between features?¶

I expected certain carriers to only operate one or a handful of flights per day but American doesn't operate any that make more than eleven trips in a day while Delta operates flights up to fourteen trips per day. Even though there were fewer delayed flights on Saturday making it a predictor for having a delay, it would not be a good predictor for the length of delay.
